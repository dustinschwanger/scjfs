Accessibility & Language Services

TTY/TDD users may call 7-1-1 for relay services. We are committed to providing accessible services and accommodations for individuals with disabilities. Bilingual services are available in Spanish. For assistance, please contact our office at (440) 998-1110.

    Contact Us

    We encourage clients to reach Child Support caseworkers through:

    • Call Center: (440) 994-1212 (Monday–Friday, 8:00 AM – 4:30 PM)
    • Web Portal: Message your caseworker directly at jfs.ohio.gov/ocs
    • Main Office: (440) 998-1110

    Make a Payment

    Payments can be made through the web portal, mobile app, or at oh.smartchildsupport.com.

    Note: If you are scheduled for genetic testing, please follow the instructions sent with your testing notice.

    Children Services

    All Children Services clients should use the Children Services front desk and lobby. If staff is assisting another client, please wait at a safe distance and we will help you as soon as possible.

    Economic Impact Payments and Child Support

    If you owe child support debt, the IRS may offset your Economic Impact Payment. Spouses who do not owe child support can file an injured spouse claim.

    • Treasury FAQ on Economic Impact Payments
    • IRS Form 8379 - Injured Spouse Allocation

  • Public Assistance Services

    To Apply for SNAP, OWF and/or Medicaid

    Online Application: www.benefits.ohio.gov

    Application is submitted online. Interviews or further contact will be by telephone and/or mail.

    To Apply for Medicaid

    Call: (440) 998-1110 or Toll-Free: (800) 935-0242

    All follow up will be by mail.

    Paper Application

    To receive a paper application for SNAP/OWF and/or Medicaid, call:

    • (440) 998-1110
    • (800) 935-0242 (Toll-Free)

    Mail completed application to:

    2924 Donahoe Drive
    Ashtabula, OH 44004

    Or fax to: (440) 998-1538

    Interviews or further contact will be by telephone and/or mail.

    Submit Required Documents

    Required documents may be:

    • Mailed to the agency address above
    • Emailed to ashtabula-verifications@jfs.ohio.gov
    • Faxed to (440) 998-1538

    Note: No in person interviews are required to apply for or receive benefits. Interviews may be required to fulfill work activity requirements for Able Bodied Adults Without Dependents (ABAWDS) or OWF applicants/recipients.

  • Workforce Services

    Job Searching on OhioMeansJobs.com

    • Job searches may be completed on OhioMeansJobs.com by entering job search (job title, employer name or zip code) and clicking on the blue "Search" button.
    • When finding a job you wish to apply for, click on the blue job title to see the full job description and how to apply.
    • To apply, follow the instructions within the job posting.

    Creating a Resume

    For individuals needing assistance with a resume:

    • Phone: (440) 994-1234
    • Email: ashtabula-verifications@jfs.ohio.gov
    • Include the following: Name, address, working phone number, valid email address, work & educational history
    • If this is too much information to provide, OhioMeansJobs.com can email you a resume template
    • OhioMeansJobs – Ashtabula County staff will create a resume for you and email a copy to your provided email address
    • 10 hard copies will be mailed to your provided address

The Public Assistance Unit offers help with day-to-day needs such as food, medical and childcare. The Child Support Enforcement Agency helps with establishing paternity, enforcement and collection of child support payments. The Public Children Services Agency oversees the protection of children and elderly, as well as foster and adoptive placements in the county. Workforce Development provides services to veterans, job seekers and employers. The Senior Services Levy provides vital support to residents age 60 and over. Adult Protective Services investigates abuse, neglect, and exploitation of vulnerable adults.
